Treasurer
The treasurer maintains all financial records and handles all financial
transactions. To ensure a smooth transition and preparation of year-end financials,
the treasurer should be available on a consulting basis for up to six months after the
fiscal year-end.
Specific duties include:
- Paying the bills
- Maintaining records of membership payments, and programs
- Depositing fees and payments received
- Reviewing and reconciling bank statements
- Drafting the budget for the coming year
- Tracking the current budget or projects on a monthly basis
- Reporting to the board on the financial affairs
- Assigning, and keeping records of donations and gifts associated projects
- Signing the annual financial report
Qualities:
- Able to commit 10 – 15 hours a month
- Organized
- Good administrative skills
- Sound understanding of financial matters
- Previous experience dealing with financial matters may be an asset
